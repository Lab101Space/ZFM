The Rules for Identifying Zero-force Members

To enable the identification of zero-force members in a truss through qualitative analysis, rather than numerical computation, a comprehensive step-by-step explanation is essential. This approach demands the application of specific rules tailored for a qualitative, node-by-node examination of the structure. The foundation for these rules lies in the principles of equilibrium conditions, which provide the necessary justification for this analytical method. Below, we detail these rules and their rationale within the framework of equilibrium.

Rule 0
Condition:The truss is not subjected to any external loads.
Conclusion:None of the truss members are subjected to any forces.
Rule 1
Condition:An unsupported and unloaded node connecting only two non-collinear truss members.
Conclusion:Neither member carries any force.
Explanation:
Since the sum of the forces acting in the y-direction must be zero, the force in member A is zero. Given that the sum of the forces in the x-direction must be zero, and the force in member A is zero, the force in member B comes out to be zero as well.
Rule 2
Condition:An unsupported and unloaded node connecting only three members two of which are collinear.
Conclusion:The other (third) member carries no force.
Explanation:
Since the sum of the forces acting in the y-direction must be zero, and the force in member C is the only force in the y-direction, it follows that C is a zero-force member.
Rule 3
Condition:An unsupported and unloaded node with a single member attached to it.
Conclusion:The member carries no force.
Explanation:
Since the sum of the forces at the node must be zero and there are no members attached to the node other than A, the force in member A is zero.
Rule 4
Condition:A member supported by a pin at each end.
Conclusion:The member carries no force.
Explanation:
The loads applied at the end nodes of the member are entirely absorbed by the pin supports; the loads are not transmitted to the member. Therefore, the member carries no force.
Rule 5
Condition:A node with a horizontal roller support, not subjected to a horizontal load, having a single horizontal member attached to it.
Conclusion:The member carries no force.
Explanation:
Since there are no horizontal forces acting at the node other than the force in member A, the force equilibrium conditions can be maintained only if the force in member A is zero.
Rule 6
Condition:An unloaded node having a horizontal roller support with a single member attached to it.
Conclusion:The member carries no force.
Explanation:
Since the sum of forces in the x-direction must be zero, and the force in member A is the only force in that direction, then A is a zero-force member.
Rule 7
Condition:A node with a vertical roller support, not subjected to a vertical load, having a single vertical member attached to it.
Conclusion:The member carries no force.
Explanation:
Since the sum of forces in the y-direction must be zero, and the force in member A is the only force in that direction, then A is a zero-force member.
Rule 8
Condition:An unloaded node having a vertical roller support with a single member attached to it.
Conclusion:The member carries no force.
Explanation:
Since the sum of forces in the y-direction must be zero, and the force in member A is the only force in that direction, then A is a zero-force member.
Rule 9
Condition:A node with a horizontal roller support and no load in the horizontal direction connects a horizontal and a vertical member.
Conclusion:The horizontal member carries no force.
Explanation:
Since the sum of forces in the x-direction must be zero, and the force in member A is the only force in that direction, then A is a zero-force member.
Rule 10
Condition:A node with a vertical roller support and no load in the vertical direction connects a horizontal and a vertical member.
Conclusion:The vertical member carries no force.
Explanation:
Since the sum of forces in the y-direction must be zero, and the force in member A is the only force in that direction, then A is a zero-force member.
Rule 11
Condition:An unsupported node, with a load in the horizontal direction, connects a horizontal and a non-horizontal member.
Conclusion:The non-horizontal member carries no force.
Explanation:
Since the sum of forces in the y-direction must be zero, and the force in member A is the only force in that direction, then A is a zero-force member.
Rule 12
Condition:An unsupported node, with a load in the vertical direction, connects a vertical and a non-vertical member.
Conclusion:The non-vertical member carries no force.
Explanation:
Since the sum of forces in the x-direction must be zero, and the force in member A is the only force in that direction, then A is a zero-force member.
Rule 13
Condition: A series of members aligned in a straight line.
(1) The series is enclosed by a pin connection at both ends. Or,
(2) The series consists of horizontal members, with a pin connection at one end and a vertical roller at the other. Or,
(3) The series consists of vertical members, with a pin connection at one end and a horizontal roller at the other.
Conclusion:None of the members in the series experiences any axial force.
Explanation:
Any existing load or force at the ends of the chain are absorbed by the supports, hence, the chain members remain force-free.
Rule 14
Condition: Two non-collinear truss members are connected to a node that is supported by a vertical roller. This node does not have any other members attached to it nor does it bear any vertical loads. Furthermore, each of these members is connected to a pin support at its opposite end.
Conclusion:Neither member carries any axial force.
Explanation:
A vertical force cannot be present at the roller support, otherwise the substructure becomes unstable as it can undego rigid-body movement in the vertical direction at the roller. Any other forces applied at the ends of the two members are absorbed by the supports, hence, the members remain force-free.
Rule 15
Condition: Two non-collinear truss members are connected to a node that is supported by a horizontal roller. This node does not have any other members attached to it nor does it bear any horizontal loads. Furthermore, each of these members is connected to a pin support at its opposite end.
Conclusion:Neither member carries any axial force.
Explanation:
A horizontal force cannot be present at the roller support, otherwise the substructure becomes unstable as it can undego rigid-body movement in the horizontal direction at the roller. Any other forces applied at the ends of the two members are absorbed by the supports, hence, the members remain force-free.
